For any embedding \(e : \mathrm{Fin}\, n \hookrightarrow \mathrm{Fin}\, L\) and any \(F\),

\[ \sum _{\sigma : \mathrm{Perm}(\mathrm{Fin}\, L)} F(\lambda u.\, \sigma (e\, u)) = (L-n)!\, \cdot \sum _{\varphi : \mathrm{Fin}\, n \hookrightarrow \mathrm{Fin}\, L} F(\lambda u.\, \varphi \, u): \]

Lean declarations

  • LeaFrontier.OrderingRecovery.independence_fillers

Conditioned on \(\hat t = 0\), the designated slots are mutually independent, with \(\mathbb {P}(X_u = a^-_{u,b}) = (1-\alpha _u)/R\) and \(\mathbb {P}(X_u = a^+_{u,b}) = \alpha _u/R\), where \(X_u\) is the slot of \(d_u\). The pairwise joint law factors: \(\mathbb {P}(X_u = s \wedge X_v = t) = \mathbb {P}(X_u=s)\, \mathbb {P}(X_v=t)\) for \(u \ne v\).

Proof ▶

Write \(g_u(s) = F_{d_u}(-s)\), supported on \(S_u\) with masses \((1-\alpha _u)/R\) and \(\alpha _u/R\). The argument has four steps. (1) Weight factorizes. Every filler has mass \(1/L\) at any actual slot, so \(w(\sigma ) = (1/L)^q \prod _{u} g_u\big((\sigma (d_u):\mathbb N)+1\big)\), with the filler factor \((1/L)^q\) a constant independent of \(\sigma \). (2) Fiber-count. By Theorem 17 applied to the designated-index embedding, summing such a functional over all \(\sigma \in \mathrm{Perm}(\mathrm{Fin}\, L)\) equals \((L-n)! = q!\) times the sum over injective placements \(\varphi \) of the \(n\) designated events. The factor \(q!\) appears in numerator and denominator and cancels. (3) Disjoint supports \(\Rightarrow \) product. By Lemma 16 the supports \(S_u\) are disjoint, so an injective placement is unconstrained beyond \(\varphi _u \in S_u\) and \(\sum _{\varphi } \prod _u g_u(\varphi _u) = \prod _u \big(\sum _{s \in S_u} g_u(s)\big)\), each inner sum \(= \sum _b\big((1-\alpha _u)/R + \alpha _u/R\big) = R \cdot (1/R) = 1\). (4) Conclude. Hence \(Z = (1/L)^q q!\), the joint law of \((X_u)\) is \(\prod _u g_u\), and the single marginals are the designated weights as claimed.

Lean declarations

  • LeaFrontier.OrderingRecovery.designated_margin

Let \(B_u\) be the block of \(X_u\); by independence the \(B_u\) are i.i.d. uniform on \(\{ 1,\dots ,R\} \). Then \(\mathbb {P}(X_u {\lt} X_v) = \frac{R(R-1)/2 + c_{uv}}{R^2}\), which equals \(\tfrac 12 + \tfrac 1{R^2}\) if \((u,v) \in A\) and \(\tfrac 12 - \tfrac 1{R^2}\) if \((v,u) \in A\). So each designated pair’s posterior majority direction is the tournament arc direction, with decision weight \(|2p-1| = 2/R^2\) and constant term \(\min (p,1-p) = \tfrac 12 - \tfrac 1{R^2}\).

Proof ▶

If \(B_u {\lt} B_v\) then \(X_u {\lt} X_v\) (earlier blocks hold smaller slots), and symmetrically for \(B_u {\gt} B_v\); if \(B_u = B_v = b\) the comparison is decided by \(\rho _b\) since within a block the consecutive slot pairs of distinct vertices do not interleave. By independence (Lemma 18),

\[ \mathbb {P}(X_u{\lt}X_v) = \mathbb {P}(B_u{\lt}B_v) + \mathbb {P}(B_u=B_v \text{ and } \rho _{B_u}(u){\lt}\rho _{B_u}(v)) = \frac{R(R-1)/2 + c_{uv}}{R^2}, \]

using Lemma 19 for the off-diagonal count. Substituting the profile margins \(c_{uv} = R/2 \pm 1\) (Lemma 13) into Lemma 20 (with \(\varepsilon = \pm 1\)) gives \(\tfrac 12 \pm \tfrac 1{R^2}\).

\(\mathbb {E}[X_u] = nR + \tfrac {2\delta _u}{R} + \alpha _u = nR + \tfrac 12 + \tfrac {\delta _u}{R^2}\).

Proof ▶

Conditional on \(B_u = b\), the expected slot of \(d_u\) is \((b-1)2n + 2\rho _b(u) - 1 + \alpha _u\). Averaging over the uniform \(B_u\),

\[ \mathbb {E}[X_u] = \tfrac 1R\sum _b\big((b-1)2n + 2\rho _b(u) - 1 + \alpha _u\big) = n(R-1) + \tfrac 2R\textstyle \sum _b\rho _b(u) - 1 + \alpha _u. \]

The rank-sum identity (Lemma 13) gives \(\tfrac 2R\sum _b\rho _b(u) = (n+1) + \tfrac {2\delta _u}{R}\), so \(\mathbb {E}[X_u] = nR + \tfrac {2\delta _u}{R} + \alpha _u\), and the definition of \(\alpha _u\) collapses this to \(nR + \tfrac 12 + \tfrac {\delta _u}{R^2}\).

Lemma 23 Rank identity
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • LeaFrontier.OrderingRecovery.rank_identity

For a permutation \(\sigma \) of \(\mathrm{Fin}\, L\), \(\sigma (x) = \# \{ i : \sigma (i) {\lt} \sigma (x)\} = \sum _i \mathbf{1}[\sigma (i) {\lt} \sigma (x)]\): a value is its own rank.

Proof ▶

Since \(\sigma \) is a bijection, the values \(\{ \sigma (i)\} \) are exactly \(\{ 0,\dots ,L-1\} \), so the number of them strictly below \(\sigma (x)\) is \(\sigma (x)\) itself.

Lean declarations

  • LeaFrontier.OrderingRecovery.filler_neutral

Every pair involving a filler has posterior probability \(1/2\) in each direction, hence (by Lemma 8) contributes exactly \(1/2\) to the cost, independent of \(\hat\pi \).

Proof ▶

Filler–filler: fillers are exchangeable (identical laws, posterior invariant under relabeling them), so \(\mathbb {P}(X_f{\lt}X_{f'}) = \mathbb {P}(X_{f'}{\lt}X_f) = \tfrac 12\), with no ties since \(\sigma \) is a permutation. Designated–filler: let \(\theta _u = \mathbb {P}(X_f {\lt} X_u)\), the same for every filler \(f\). The pointwise rank identity (Lemma 23) \(X_u = 1 + \sum _{v \ne u}\mathbf{1}[X_v{\lt}X_u] + \sum _{r}\mathbf{1}[X_{f_r}{\lt}X_u]\) gives, in expectation, \(\mathbb {E}[X_u] = 1 + \sum _{v\ne u}\mathbb {P}(X_v{\lt}X_u) + q\, \theta _u\). By the margins (Lemma 21) and the degree sum (Lemma 24), \(\sum _{v\ne u}\mathbb {P}(X_v{\lt}X_u) = \tfrac {n-1}{2} + \tfrac {\delta _u}{R^2}\). Substituting \(\mathbb {E}[X_u] = nR + \tfrac 12 + \tfrac {\delta _u}{R^2}\) (Lemma 22) and \(q = 2nR - n\) yields \(q\, \theta _u = nR - \tfrac n2 = q/2\), so \(\theta _u = \tfrac 12\).
