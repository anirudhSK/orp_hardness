Lemma 9 Single-arc gadget margins and rank sums
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 1
  • Definition 1

Lean declarations

  • LeaFrontier.OrderingRecovery.gadget_arc

For an arc \((u,v)\) pick any order \(\omega \) of \(V \setminus \{ u,v\} \) and form the two orders \(O_1 = (u,v,\omega )\) and \(O_2 = (\omega ^{\operatorname {rev}}, u, v)\). Then: (i) both rank \(u\) before \(v\); (ii) every ordered pair other than the arc itself (in either orientation) is reversed between \(O_1\) and \(O_2\) (net margin \(0\)); (iii) the two orders give \(u\) the 0-indexed rank sum \(n-2\) (ranks \(0\) and \(n-2\)), \(v\) the rank sum \(n\) (ranks \(1\) and \(n-1\)), and every \(x \notin \{ u,v\} \) the rank sum \(n-1\).

Proof ▶

In both \(O_1\) and \(O_2\), \(u\) is ranked before \(v\), giving clause (i). For clause (ii): pairs internal to \(\omega \) are reversed by construction (one order uses \(\omega \), the other \(\omega ^{\operatorname {rev}}\)); and for each \(x \in \omega \), both \(u\) and \(v\) precede \(x\) in \(O_1\) while \(x\) precedes both in \(O_2\), so the pairs \(\{ u,x\} \) and \(\{ v,x\} \) are reversed as well. Thus every pair except \(\{ u,v\} \) itself flips orientation between \(O_1\) and \(O_2\), contributing net margin \(0\). For clause (iii): \(u\) has ranks \(0\) and \(n-2\), \(v\) has ranks \(1\) and \(n-1\), and each \(x \in \omega \) occupies symmetric positions reversed between the two orders, summing to \(n-1\). (The note states this 1-indexed as rank sums \(n\), \(n+2\), \(n+1\); subtracting \(2\) for the two orders gives the 0-indexed form used in Lean.)

Definition 10 Profile construction
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 1
  • Lemma 9

Lean declarations

  • LeaFrontier.OrderingRecovery.profileMap

Set \(m = \binom n2\) and \(R = 2m + 8n\). The profile \(\rho : \mathrm{Fin}\, R \to \mathrm{Perm}(\mathrm{Fin}\, n)\) is the concatenation of: (a) for each of the \(m\) arcs \((u,v) \in A\), the two gadget orders \(O_1, O_2\) of Lemma 9 (\(2m\) orders, extracted by choice); (b) \(4n\) copies of the identity and \(4n\) copies of its reverse \(\mathrm{rev}\) (the neutral pairs \((\tau , \tau ^{\operatorname {rev}})\)). Ranks are 0-indexed, \((\rho _b(u) : \mathbb {N}) \in \{ 0,\dots ,n-1\} \). This is hand-authored in Lean as a choice-based construction; the development tracks \(R = 2m + 8n\) directly rather than proving \(m = \binom n2\) (downstream only needs even / \({\ge }\, 8n\) / \({\le }\, 2n^2+8n\)).

Lemma 11 Profile margins
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 10
  • Lemma 9
  • Lemma 9
  • Definition 10

Lean declarations

  • LeaFrontier.OrderingRecovery.profile_margins

For ordered \(u \ne v\), \(c_{uv} := |\{ b : \rho _b(u) {\lt} \rho _b(v)\} |\) equals \(R/2 + 1\) if \((u,v) \in A\) and \(R/2 - 1\) if \((v,u) \in A\).

Proof ▶

The gadget for arc \((u,v)\) contributes \(2\) to its own pair \(u\)-before-\(v\) and, by Lemma 9(ii), exactly \(1\) to every other unordered pair (one of \(O_1,O_2\) ranks them each way). Every other arc’s gadget contributes \(1\) to the pair \(\{ u,v\} \), and each of the \(4n\) neutral pairs contributes \(1\). Summing for a pair with \((u,v) \in A\): \(2 + (m-1) + 4n = m + 1 + 4n = R/2 + 1\); for \((v,u) \in A\) it is \(0 + (m-1) + 4n + 1 = R/2 - 1\).

Lemma 12 Profile rank sums
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 10
  • Lemma 2
  • Lemma 9

Lean declarations

  • LeaFrontier.OrderingRecovery.profile_ranksums

For every vertex \(u\), \(\sum _b \rho _b(u) = R(n+1)/2 + \delta _u\) (1-indexed); in Lean the equivalent 0-indexed doubled form \(2\sum _b (\rho _b u : \mathbb {N}) = R(n-1) + 2\delta _u\).

Proof ▶

By Lemma 9(iii), in each arc gadget vertex \(u\) has rank sum \(n-2\) (0-indexed) when it is the tail of that arc, \(n\) when the head, and \(n-1\) otherwise; each neutral pair gives every vertex rank sum \(n-1\). With \(\operatorname {outdeg}(u)\) tails and \(\operatorname {indeg}(u)\) heads among the \(m\) gadgets, the total 0-indexed rank sum is \((n-1)(m + 4n) + (\operatorname {indeg}(u) - \operatorname {outdeg}(u)) = (n-1)R/2 + \delta _u\). Doubling and rearranging gives the stated identity.

There is an even \(R = 2m + 8n \ge 8n\) and a profile \(\rho : \mathrm{Fin}\, R \to \mathrm{Perm}(\mathrm{Fin}\, n)\) satisfying both the margin identity (Lemma 11) and the rank-sum identity (Lemma 12). This packages Definition 10 with the two preceding lemmas into the existential \(\exists R\, \rho ,\ \mathrm{Even}\, R \wedge 8n \le R \wedge R = 2m+8n \wedge \text{margins} \wedge \text{ranksums}\) that everything downstream consumes via .choose.

Proof ▶

Take \(R = 2m + 8n\), which is even and \(\ge 8n\), and \(\rho = \) the profile of Definition 10. The margin and rank-sum conjuncts are Lemmas 11 and 12; the evenness and size facts are immediate from \(R = 2m+8n\).
